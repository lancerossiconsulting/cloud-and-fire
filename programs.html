The Fellowship Cohort

A Practice of Listening, Built for the Local Church

Structured formation for church leaders who want to hear what their congregations are actually carrying — and how to act on what they hear.

What a Cohort Is

Each fellowship cohort brings together a small group of church leaders for formation in structured listening and facilitation. The goal is not a program you add to your calendar; it is a practice you carry back into your ministry. Fellows leave equipped to host dialogue, hold deliberation, and surface real discernment within their own congregations.

The cohort model is interdenominational by design. Baptist and Catholic, mainline and evangelical, rural congregation and urban parish — the shared process is the constant; the theological and ecclesial context is each church's own.

The Three-Phase Session Format

Every fellowship session moves through three distinct phases, each suited to a different kind of work. Trained facilitators hold the boundaries between them — ensuring the group doesn't collapse into debate before it has listened, or rush to action before it has deliberated.

Phase 1

Dialogue

Builds trust and surfaces the full range of perspectives in the room. No pressure toward resolution. The goal is to hear — before anyone begins to persuade.

Phase 2

Deliberation

Introduces honest framing of tradeoffs and competing values. Participants weigh options against their own convictions rather than reacting to positions.

Phase 3

Discernment & Action

Where appropriate, identifies concrete next steps the congregation or its leadership can take in response to what has been heard.

The Research Component

Each cohort carries a single driving question — not assigned by the fellowship, but one that emerges from the congregation's own discourse. Fellows document what surfaces in their dialogues, contribute findings to a shared body of learning, and strengthen future cohorts and partner churches through what they discover.

Over time, this cumulative documentation forms the basis for the fellowship's academic publications — structured inquiry into how faith communities communicate when the stakes are real.

What Participating Leaders Receive

  • Formation in structured facilitation, Christian listening, and discernment practices
  • A repeatable process for hosting dialogue within your own congregation
  • Concise summaries of major themes, recurring questions, and unresolved tensions from your sessions
  • A cohort of peers navigating the same work across different church contexts
  • Practical ministry intelligence — surfaced from your own people, in their own words

Cohort Details

Details for the inaugural cohort — including schedule, format, time commitment, and cost — will be confirmed and shared directly with church leaders who have expressed interest. If you are exploring whether this is the right fit for your congregation, the best next step is a conversation.
